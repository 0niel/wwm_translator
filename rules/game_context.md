# Where Winds Meet - Game Context

## Overview

**Where Winds Meet** (燕云十六声 / Yan Yun Sixteen Sounds) is an epic open-world action-adventure RPG rooted in the rich legacy of Wuxia. Set during the turbulent era of tenth-century China, players take on the role of a young sword master, uncovering forgotten truths and the mysteries of their own identity. As the wind stirs across mountains and rivers, so too will their legend rise.

## Setting

- **Time Period**: Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms (五代十国, 907-979 CE)
- **Location**: Ancient China - Yan Yun region (modern Beijing area), imperial capitals, wilderness
- **Genre**: Wuxia (武侠) martial arts fiction + Open-world Action RPG
- **Historical Context**: Era of political fragmentation, rise and fall of dynasties, martial arts flourishing

## Key Features

### An Era on the Brink. A Hero on the Rise.
Immerse yourself in the captivating Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period of China, where epic battles and legendary tales come to life. From the bustling imperial capital to the hidden wilderness, every path is filled with secrets and adventures. Freedom carries weight—stir up mischief or take the noble route: befriend villagers, forge alliances, and carve your legacy as a Wuxia hero.

### An Open World of Infinite Possibilities
Explore a vibrant world filled with life, from bustling cities to hidden temples in lush forests—all dynamically shaped by time, weather, and player choices. Experience Wuxia-style traversal: scale rooftops, glide through the air, or use fast travel between regions. Uncover thousands of points of interest across 20+ unique regions.

### Master Your Way of Wuxia Combat
Tailor combat approach—melee, ranged, or stealth. Engage in combat with classic Wuxia weapons: swords, spears, dual blades, rope dart, bows, fans, and umbrellas. Switch weapons and employ martial arts and mystic skills like Tai Chi to outmaneuver enemies.

### Adventure Alone or Forge Your Community
Embark on a narrative-driven adventure filled with rich solo gameplay, or team up with up to four friends for seamless co-op experiences. Join or create a Guild to access group activities—intense guild wars, multiplayer dungeons, and epic raids.

## Core Themes

### Martial Arts (Wuxia 武侠)
- **Martial arts schools/sects** (门派 menpai) - organized groups teaching specific styles
- **Internal energy cultivation** (内功 neigong) - developing internal power through meditation and practice
- **Qi** (气) - life force energy flowing through body and nature
- **Master-student relationships** (师徒 shi-tu) - sacred bond of teaching and learning
- **Legendary weapons** (神兵 shenbing) - mystical arms with special powers
- **Martial techniques** (武学 wuxue) - combat skills and secret arts
- **Lightness skill** (轻功 qinggong) - ability to move with superhuman agility

### Chinese Culture & Philosophy
- **Confucianism** (儒家): Hierarchy, loyalty, honor, righteousness (义)
- **Taoism** (道家): Harmony with nature, balance, wu-wei (non-action)
- **Buddhism** (佛教): Karma, enlightenment, monasteries
- **Poetry & Literature**: Classical Chinese poetry, calligraphy
- **Tea Culture**: Tea ceremonies as meditation and social bonding
- **Traditional Medicine**: Herbs, acupuncture, balance of elements
- **Festivals**: Lunar New Year, Mid-Autumn, Qingming

### Political & Social Structure
- **Imperial Court** (朝廷): Emperor, ministers, eunuchs
- **Jianghu** (江湖): "Rivers and Lakes" - martial underworld society
- **Nobility & Officials**: Complex hierarchy, titles, etiquette
- **Common Folk**: Peasants, merchants, craftsmen
- **Outlaws & Bandits**: Those outside the law

## Tone & Style Guidelines

### By Content Type

**UI/System Messages**
- Concise, clear, imperative mood
- Gaming terminology (familiar to Russian players)
- Example: "Quest completed" → "Задание выполнено" (not "Вы успешно завершили квест")

**Quest Text**
- Natural narrative flow
- Atmospheric, immersive
- Maintain urgency or mood of original
- Example: "Investigate the mysterious disappearance" → "Расследуйте загадочное исчезновение"

**Dialogue**
- **Formal** (к старшим): вы, Вы, Ваш, с почтением
- **Informal** (к младшим/равным): ты, твой
- **Imperial/Court**: высокопарный, архаичный стиль
- **Common folk**: простой, живой язык
- **Martial artists**: терминология ушу, кодекс чести

**Lore & History**
- Elevated, slightly archaic style
- Respectful tone for historical/cultural content
- Preserve Chinese concepts, explain if needed

**Combat & Skills**
- Dynamic, impactful verbs
- Short, punchy descriptions for skills
- Example: "Strike with the force of thunder" → "Удар с силой грома"

**Item Descriptions**
- Evocative, poetic for legendary items
- Practical for common items
- Cultural authenticity

## Cultural Translation Notes

### Honorifics & Address
- Keep Chinese titles when culturally significant (Shifu, Daxia)
- Transliterate + explain in context when first used
- Use Russian equivalents for generic titles (lord → господин)

### Names
- **Chinese names**: Transliterate using Russian Pinyin system
  - 李白 (Li Bai) → Ли Бай
  - 王维 (Wang Wei) → Ван Вэй
- **Place names**: Transliterate, add descriptive if needed
  - 燕云 (Yan Yun) → Янь Юнь
- **Technique names**: Translate meaning + keep Chinese feel
  - 太极剑 → Меч Тайцзи
  - 降龙十八掌 → Восемнадцать ладоней покорения дракона

### Philosophical Concepts
- **Dao** (道) → Дао / Путь (context-dependent)
- **De** (德) → Добродетель
- **Ren** (仁) → Человечность / Милосердие
- **Yi** (义) → Справедливость / Долг чести
- **Li** (礼) → Ритуал / Этикет

### Avoid Common Pitfalls
- ❌ Don't translate "Jianghu" as just "world" → Use "Цзянху" or "мир боевых искусств"
- ❌ Don't use modern slang in historical context
- ❌ Don't Westernize names (keep Chinese transcription)
- ❌ Don't lose martial arts terminology depth
- ✅ Preserve the Wuxia atmosphere and terminology
- ✅ Keep cultural authenticity while being understandable
